# LV-013 - Flux Gallery

1. **Block A** uses the unit cube \(0 \le x,y,z \le 1\). Applying the Divergence Theorem:
   - \(F_1\): \(\nabla \cdot F_1 = 1 + 2 + 2 = 5 \Rightarrow \Phi = 5\).
   - \(F_2\): \(\nabla \cdot F_2 = 2 + 3 + 6 = 11 \Rightarrow \Phi = 11\).
   - \(F_3\): \(\nabla \cdot F_3 = 10x + 15\). Integrating over the cube gives \(10 \int_0^1 x dx + 15 = 5 + 15 = 20\).
   - \(F_4\): \(\nabla \cdot F_4 = 16x + 10y + 10\). Integrating yields \(16 \cdot \tfrac{1}{2} + 10 \cdot \tfrac{1}{2} + 10 = 23\).
   Mapping \(0 \rightarrow a\) produces letters `flux`.
2. **Block B** covers \(0 \le x,z \le 1\) and \(0 \le y \le 2\).
   - \(G_1\): divergence \(= 4\) ⇒ flux \(4 \cdot \text{vol} = 4 \cdot 2 = 8\).
   - \(G_2\): divergence \(= 4 + \tfrac{5}{2}y\). Integrating: \(4 \cdot 2 + \tfrac{5}{2} \int_0^2 y dy = 8 + 5 = 13\).
   - \(G_3\): divergence \(= \tfrac{7}{2} + 3y + 5x + z\). Integrating term by term gives \(7 + 6 + 5 + 1 = 19\).
   - \(G_4\): divergence \(= 2\) ⇒ flux \(= 4\).
   These numbers map to `inte`.
3. **Block C** spans \(0 \le x,y \le 1, 0 \le z \le 2\).
   - \(H_1\): divergence \(= 3\) ⇒ flux \(= 6\).
   - \(H_2\): divergence \(= 5 + 5x + 2y\). Integrating gives \(10 + 5 + 2 = 17\).
   - \(H_3\): divergence \(= 0\) ⇒ flux \(= 0\).
   - \(H_4\): divergence \(= \tfrac{7}{2} + 3y + \tfrac{1}{2}z\). Integration yields \(7 + 3 + 1 = 11\).
   These map to `gral`.
